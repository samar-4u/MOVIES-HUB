Storyline

"Peaky Blinders" is a British crime drama set in post-World War I Birmingham, England, following the exploits of the Shelby crime family. The story centers on **Thomas Shelby**, a decorated war hero turned ruthless gangster, as he seeks to expand the family's influence and power in a city rife with political corruption, rival gangs, and police forces. The name "Peaky Blinders" comes from the group's trademark razor-blade-infused flat caps, which they use as weapons. Over the course of six seasons, the Shelbys face internal conflicts, betrayals, and threats from rival criminal enterprises, all while navigating the complex social and political landscape of 1920s Britain. With its dark tone, stylish cinematography, and gritty realism, the series delves into themes of loyalty, family, ambition, and the lasting effects of war.
