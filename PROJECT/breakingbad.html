Storyline

"Breaking Bad" follows Walter White, a high school chemistry teacher in Albuquerque, New Mexico, who turns to manufacturing and selling methamphetamine after being diagnosed with terminal cancer. Faced with the pressures of securing his family's financial future and grappling with his increasing desperation, Walter partners with a former student, Jesse Pinkman, in the illegal drug trade. As Walter descends deeper into the criminal underworld, he adopts the alias "Heisenberg," becoming a ruthless drug kingpin. The show explores themes of morality, crime, family, and the consequences of choices. Walter's transformation from a mild-mannered teacher to a feared criminal mastermind is at the heart of the series.
