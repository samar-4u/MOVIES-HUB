Storyline

"Dangal" is based on the true story of **Mahavir Singh Phogat**, an Indian wrestler who trains his daughters, **Geeta** and **Babita Phogat**, to become world-class wrestlers. Despite facing immense societal pressure, family challenges, and gender bias in a male-dominated sport, Mahavir does not relent in his quest to make his daughters successful wrestlers. The film begins with Mahavir's failed wrestling career and his dream of seeing his children excel in the sport. When his dreams of having a son to continue his wrestling legacy are not realized, he decides to train his daughters instead, believing they have the potential to achieve greatness. The story follows the Phogat family as they fight their own battles, both on the wrestling mat and in their personal lives. **Geeta** eventually becomes India's first female wrestler to win a gold medal at the Commonwealth Games, while **Babita** too achieves great success. The film is a tribute to the determination, sacrifices, and unbreakable bond of a father and his daughters who defy all odds to achieve their dreams.
